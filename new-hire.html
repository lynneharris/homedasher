Role Details

What to Expect as a
HomeDasher

Everything you need to know before you apply — what the work looks like, how scheduling works, and what we provide.

Compensation

HomeDashers are W-2 employees — whether you work occasional shifts or a full schedule. You get the flexibility of a gig platform, choosing the jobs that fit your life, while receiving the stability and benefits of real employment. No 1099s, no uncertainty. Compensation is competitive and discussed during the interview process.

🗓️

Day to Day

No two shifts look the same — that's part of the job. You might spend one morning prepping meals and organizing a kitchen, and the next afternoon handling laundry, changing beds, and researching a contractor for a client.

Before you arrive, you'll receive a fully custom to-do list built by the HomeDasher Planner — our task intake system. It tells you exactly what needs doing, where things are, and how the client likes things done. No orientation, no guesswork. You arrive ready to work.

Shifts range from 1 hour to a full day, depending on the client's booking. You choose which jobs to accept.

HomeDashers handle a wide range of household tasks, including:

  • Cleaning — surfaces, mirrors, toilets, dishes, fridge, trash, sweeping and vacuuming
  • Laundry — wash, dry, fold, put away; changing and making beds
  • Meal prep — grocery lists, ingredient prep, simple cooking from client recipes, pantry organization
  • Tidying and organizing — any room, closets, drawers, decluttering
  • Home admin — phone calls, scheduling, research, emails, forms, digital organization
  • Pet and plant care — feeding, litter, watering, medication

Comfort with both physical and digital tasks is important — a single shift might include organizing a pantry and then looking something up on your phone for a client.

You set your availability and choose which jobs you accept — there's no fixed schedule imposed on you. When a job opens up in your area that matches your availability, you'll be notified and can accept or pass.

The more availability you offer, the more jobs you'll see. There's no minimum number of hours required to remain active as a HomeDasher.

🎓

Training & Support

Before your first independent visit, you'll complete a hands-on training process that includes working alongside an experienced HomeDasher manager. This apprenticeship is designed to build your confidence across all task types — cleaning, admin, meal prep, and more — so you're fully prepared before you go solo.

You'll also learn how to use the HomeDasher Planner so you know exactly how to read and execute a custom to-do list.

It happens — that's part of what makes this role exciting. HomeDashers are expected to think on their feet and adapt. If something's unclear, you use your best judgment, make a decision, and document what you did for the client.

Support is always available if you run into something genuinely outside your abilities. But the role is designed for people who can problem-solve independently — if you need step-by-step guidance for every situation, this might not be the right fit.

✅

Requirements

  • Located in the Greater Seattle Area
  • Reliable transportation to client homes
  • Smartphone for job notifications, task lists, and admin work
  • Able to pass a background check
  • Eligible to work in the US

Prior experience in housekeeping, cleaning, home organization, personal assisting, or household management is a strong plus — but not required. What matters most is that you're the kind of person who notices what needs doing, takes initiative, and delivers high-quality work without needing to be told how to do it.

We're looking for people with great instincts, a strong work ethic, and genuine pride in what they do. The rest can be trained.

HomeDasher provides you with a professional cleaning kit — sanitizing spray, glass cleaner, stainless steel cleaner, paper towels, and disposable toilet wands. For everything else, you'll use what's already in the client's home.

For admin and research tasks, you use your own phone or laptop. You never access a client's computer or personal devices.
